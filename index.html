🗺️ National Parks & Wildlife Reserves

Six of the world's great wildlife destinations — from America's first national park to the Galápagos, where Darwin's theory of evolution was born.

🦬 🇺🇸
North America

Yellowstone National Park

📍 Wyoming, USA — est. 1872
🦬 Bison 🐺 Wolves 🐻 Grizzlies 🦅 Bald Eagle

The world's first national park and "America's Serengeti." Home to the continent's largest free-roaming mammal population — wolves were controversially reintroduced in 1995 and have become one of conservation's greatest success stories.

Visit official site ↗
🦓 🇹🇿
East Africa

Serengeti National Park

📍 Tanzania — UNESCO World Heritage Site
🦁 Big Five 🦓 Great Migration 🐆 Cheetah

Home to the Great Migration — two million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles thundering across 5,700 square miles in what's called the greatest wildlife spectacle on Earth. The Big Five are year-round residents.

Visit official site ↗
🐘 🇿🇦
Southern Africa

Kruger National Park

📍 South Africa — 5 million acres
🦁 Big Five 🦏 Rhino 🐊 Crocodile

Africa's most iconic safari park — 5 million acres with one of the highest densities of wildlife on the continent. Self-drive safaris, luxury lodges, and 147 mammal species including all of the Big Five.

Visit official site ↗
🐢 🇪🇨
South Pacific

Galápagos National Park

📍 Ecuador — where Darwin changed biology
🐢 Giant Tortoise 🦎 Marine Iguana 🐧 Penguin

A living laboratory of evolution — the archipelago that inspired Darwin's theory of natural selection. Giant tortoises, marine iguanas, and blue-footed boobies found nowhere else on Earth. Species here have no fear of humans.

Visit official site ↗
🦍 🇷🇼
Central Africa

Volcanoes National Park

📍 Rwanda — gorilla trekking capital
🦍 Mountain Gorilla 🐒 Golden Monkey 🦁 Buffalo

The world's most significant mountain gorilla habitat — fewer than 1,100 survive in the wild, and Volcanoes protects a large share of them. Trekking through dense mist-forest to sit with a gorilla family is regarded as one of the world's great wildlife experiences.

Visit official site ↗
🌿 🇧🇷
South America

Amazon National Park

📍 Brazil — world's largest rainforest
🐆 Jaguar 🐊 Caiman 🦜 Macaw 🐬 River Dolphin

The Amazon is home to an estimated 10% of all species on Earth — jaguars, pink river dolphins, electric eels, poison dart frogs, and millions of insects yet to be named. The world's lungs and one of its greatest wildlife frontiers.

Visit official site ↗
🏔️ 🇨🇦
Canada

Banff National Park

📍 Alberta — est. 1885 · Canada's first national park
🐻 Grizzly Bear 🦌 Elk 🐺 Wolf 🐐 Mountain Goat

Home to 56 species of mammals, 300 species of birds, and more than 800 species of plants across glacial lakes, soaring peaks, and dense coniferous forest. It has the most wildlife crossings — 38 underpasses and 6 overpasses — in the world.

Visit official site ↗
🌌 🇨🇦
Canada

Jasper National Park

📍 Alberta — UNESCO World Heritage Site · Dark Sky Preserve
🦌 Caribou 🫎 Moose 🐻 Black Bear 🦊 Arctic Fox

Canada's largest Rocky Mountain national park and one of the world's largest Dark Sky Preserves — where caribou, moose, and wolves roam beneath skies unobstructed by light pollution. Shares the Icefields Parkway with Banff, one of the world's great wildlife drives.

Visit official site ↗
🐯 🇮🇳
India

Jim Corbett National Park

📍 Uttarakhand — Asia's first national park (1936)
🐯 Bengal Tiger 🐘 Asian Elephant 🐆 Leopard 🦅 600+ bird species

Asia's first national park, established in 1936, with one of the world's highest densities of Bengal tigers — over 260 recorded in the park. Named after the legendary conservationist-hunter Jim Corbett, and the birthplace of Project Tiger in 1973.

Visit official site ↗
🦏 🇮🇳
India

Kaziranga National Park

📍 Assam — UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1985
🦏 One-Horned Rhino 🐯 Bengal Tiger 🐘 Asian Elephant 🦬 Wild Buffalo

Holds about two-thirds of the world's wild one-horned rhinos — 2,613 individuals as of 2022, the highest count ever recorded. Also has one of the world's highest tiger densities, and the world's largest population of wild water buffalo.

Visit official site ↗
🏯 🇮🇳
India

Ranthambore National Park

📍 Rajasthan — tigers among ancient Mughal ruins
🐯 Bengal Tiger 🐆 Leopard 🦌 Sambar Deer 🐊 Marsh Crocodile

One of the most famous tiger reserves in India — where Bengal tigers are reliably spotted in the open, often photographed resting in the shadow of 10th-century fort ruins. One of the few places in the world where tigers can be seen in such dramatic historical landscapes.

Visit official site ↗
🦎 🇮🇩
Southeast Asia

Komodo National Park

📍 Indonesia — exclusive home of the Komodo dragon
🦎 Komodo Dragon 🦈 Reef Shark 🐢 Sea Turtle 🦅 Orange-footed Scrubfowl

Home to the Komodo dragon — the world's largest living lizard, capable of taking down a water buffalo. Above the waterline it's a land of prehistoric reptiles; below it, some of the most biodiverse coral reefs and marine life on the planet.

Visit official site ↗
